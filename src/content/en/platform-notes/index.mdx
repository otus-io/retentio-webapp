# Retentio Platform Description

## 1. Platform Overview

Retentio — from the Latin <em>retentio</em>, meaning retention or the act of holding what you have learned in memory — is a collaborative learning platform built on **spaced repetition**. People create, update, and improve decks together: building cards, sharing decks, correcting errors, refining wording, and keeping content current as a subject evolves. The goal is a living library of high-quality decks, continuously improved by the people who know the material best.

At its core, Retentio applies decades of cognitive research on the spacing effect and the forgetting curve. Instead of cramming or reviewing everything every day, you review each item when it is due; intervals grow longer when you remember easily and shorten when you struggle. You focus on answering; Retentio handles the timing.

## 2. Product Positioning

Retentio is positioned for learners who need durable long-term retention — language vocabulary, medical and professional terminology, exam preparation, and ongoing knowledge building — without the friction that often comes with legacy spaced-repetition tools.

The platform addresses two problems that motivated its creation:

- **Complexity before learning** — Many SRS apps require imports, add-ons, and extensive configuration before the first productive review. Retentio is designed to be simple from day one: open a deck and start reviewing.
- **Uneven shared content** — Public flashcard sets are often full of typos, outdated facts, and cards that test the wrong thing. Retentio treats decks as shared, improvable artifacts rather than static downloads.

Retentio combines structured flashcard study with collaborative maintenance and scheduling designed for sustainable daily use — not short-term cramming or unsustainable review backlogs.

## 3. Core Features

### 3.1 Decks and Flashcard Learning

Users create and organize decks of flashcards — questions and answers, definitions, terms, example sentences, and other structured formats — and study through spaced review sessions.

### 3.2 Collaborative Deck Building

Anyone can build a deck, share it, and invite others to contribute. Decks improve over time as contributors add cards, fix inaccuracies, and refine prompts — so quality and accuracy compound rather than decay.

### 3.3 Intelligent Review Scheduling

Retentio uses scheduling algorithms built on spacing science, designed to avoid the backlogs and setup burden common in classical SRS tools:

- **Sustainable review load** — intervals stay manageable as decks grow, without snowballing daily queues.
- **Simple from day one** — no add-on ecosystem or configuration rabbit holes.
- **Adaptive to how you recall** — each card’s next due date reflects your actual performance on that item.
- **Deck-aware pacing** — scheduling can reflect the material and intended pace of a deck (e.g. medical terminology vs. language vocabulary vs. exam prep).

### 3.4 Learning Progress Tracking

The platform records study sessions, review completion, and mastery status so learners can see progress over time and keep a steady daily rhythm instead of last-minute cramming.

### 3.5 AI-Assisted Deck Quality

Educator and community judgment set the standard; AI helps maintain quality at scale. A review pipeline runs across published decks to surface issues that are easy to miss during manual authoring:

- **Accuracy checks** — factual claims that conflict with up-to-date sources
- **Clarity rewrites** — ambiguous or double-barrelled prompts
- **Redundancy detection** — cards that test the same fact in slightly different words
- **Difficulty calibration** — items that are harder or easier than their position in the deck suggests
- **Coverage gaps** — concepts missing from the deck’s stated scope

Suggested edits are reviewed before they go live; authors and educators stay in control.

## 4. Scheduling and Science

Retentio’s scheduling is grounded in the spacing effect — one of the most replicated findings in cognitive psychology — and Ebbinghaus’s forgetting curve: retention drops quickly after learning unless review is timed to reinforce memory just before it fades.

Classical algorithms such as SM-2 automated intervals but often left users with steep setup, prediction-focused schedules, and review loads that became unsustainable. Retentio’s algorithms target those gaps while preserving the core principle: review what needs review, skip what you already know, and lengthen intervals as memories stabilize.

After each review:

- If you remembered easily, the interval increases.
- If you struggled or failed, the interval shortens or resets.

## 5. Target Users

Retentio is intended for:

- **Students at all levels** who want to improve learning efficiency through spaced, systematic review
- **Ph.D. and medical students** and other advanced learners memorizing dense terminology or concepts
- **Language learners** building vocabulary and expressions for long-term use
- **Exam and certification candidates** who need systematic review rather than cramming
- **Self-directed learners** maintaining a personal knowledge base over months or years
- **Educators** who want to publish decks shaped by classroom experience — where each card tests one idea, wording is clear, and sequencing builds from foundations outward

High-quality educator-built decks benefit not only their authors’ students but every learner on the platform who studies the same subject.

## 6. Platform Value

Retentio is designed to replace common failure modes in both traditional study and legacy SRS tools:

- Content forgotten soon after cramming
- Disorganized or unsustainable review schedules
- Time lost configuring tools instead of learning
- Shared decks that are inaccurate, outdated, or poorly written
- Repetitive review of items already mastered

By pairing spaced repetition with collaborative deck quality and scheduling built for daily sustainability, Retentio helps learners spend time on material that actually needs reinforcement — and on decks that improve over time.

## 7. Use Cases

Typical scenarios include:

- Language vocabulary and expressions (e.g. English, Japanese, and other L2 study)
- Medical, legal, finance, programming, and other professional terminology
- Academic and certification exam review
- Personal reading notes and long-term knowledge management
- Community-maintained decks that stay accurate as fields update

Retentio is a collaborative spaced-repetition platform: simple to start, grounded in retention science, and built so decks and schedules stay usable over the long term.
