# Show Answer

## Show Answer

Show Answer is a core step in the Retentio learning process. After users start learning, the system displays the cards that need to be studied or reviewed based on the learning algorithm.

In most cases, the card first shows the front side. Users need to look at the information displayed on the front side and try to recall the corresponding answer. After recalling the answer, users can click the card or click the button at the bottom of the page to show the answer.

This process helps users actively recall the learning content instead of simply viewing the result directly.

## Card Front

During learning, the system first displays the front side of a card. The front side usually shows one or more fields and their corresponding content.

For example, in an English learning deck, the front side of a card may display:

`English: hello`

The user needs to recall the corresponding Chinese meaning based on this content.

In other learning scenarios, the front side of a card may also display a concept, question, image, audio, or other field content. What appears on the front side depends on the field settings of the deck and the way the card is generated.

## Recalling the Answer

Before showing the answer, users should first try to recall the answer by themselves. This is an important part of memory training.

For example, when the front side displays:

`English: hello`

The user should try to recall the meaning, such as:

`中文: 你好`

By doing this, users can actively check whether they have truly understood and remembered the fact, instead of only feeling that the content looks familiar.

## Show Answer Operation

After users have tried to recall the answer, they can click the card or click the button at the bottom of the page to show the answer.

After the answer is shown, the card displays the back side content, which helps users check whether their recall was correct. For example:

Front: `English: hello`  
Back: `中文: 你好`

Users can compare the answer they recalled with the answer shown on the back side of the card, and then judge whether they have mastered the fact.

## Checking Mastery

The purpose of showing the answer is not only to display the result, but also to help users evaluate their mastery of the current fact.

If users can recall the answer correctly, it means the fact already has a certain memory foundation. If users cannot recall the answer, or if the recalled answer is incomplete, it means the fact still needs more learning and review.

This judgment can affect the following learning process. The system can use the user’s feedback on the card to adjust when and how often the card appears again, making the review process better match the user’s actual memory state.

## Summary

Show Answer is an important step in the Retentio learning process. The system displays the card that currently needs to be studied based on the learning algorithm. Users first recall the answer according to the field content shown on the front side, and then click the card or the page button to show the answer.

By showing the answer, users can check whether they have mastered the corresponding fact and provide a basis for later learning feedback and review scheduling. This makes learning more than just reading content. It becomes an active recall and memory training process.
