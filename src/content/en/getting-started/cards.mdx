# Cards

## Cards

Cards are the core learning units in Retentio and the main content users interact with during daily study and review. A complete deck is usually made up of many cards, and by studying these cards over time, users gradually build memory and mastery within a specific topic or area of knowledge.

## Front and Back

The concept of cards comes from real-world knowledge flashcards. In most cases, a card has two sides: a front and a back. The front is typically used to present a question, prompt, or something the user needs to recall, while the back is used to show the answer, explanation, or additional information.

This structure encourages active recall before revealing the result, which leads to a more effective memory training process.

## Content Types

In Retentio, both the front and back of a card can contain not only text, but also images and audio. By supporting multiple content types, Retentio can be used in a wider range of learning scenarios, such as language learning, terminology memorization, image recognition, or pronunciation training.

This flexible structure makes cards suitable not only for simple question-and-answer memorization, but also for more complex and expressive forms of learning content.

## Relation to Facts

In Retentio, cards are not always created entirely by hand. Instead, they are generated from facts created by the user.

For example, when a user creates a fact with the fields `English: hello` and `Chinese: 你好`, the system will, by default, automatically generate a card. The front of the card will display `hello`, and the back will display `你好`.

In this way, users only need to organize and enter fact-based content first, while the system can generate the corresponding study cards according to predefined rules, making content creation and learning organization more efficient.

## Summary

Cards are the most essential learning carriers in Retentio. They follow the traditional front-and-back flashcard structure while supporting multiple types of content, including text, images, and audio. At the same time, cards can be automatically generated from facts, allowing users to organize learning materials and carry out memory training in a more efficient and structured way.
